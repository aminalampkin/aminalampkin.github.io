Meet Amina Symone

I want to capture your most powerful moments. My name is Amina Symone Lampkin. Born and raised in Connecticut, I've always had a passion for reading and writing. Knowing my family's history, as well as the history of the Black experience in America, I have been immersed into the art of storytelling.

When I turned 16, I picked up my first camera: Canon Powershot SX520. It started with me taking photos of clothing samples my best friend designed. After that first photoshoot, I knew photography was something I wanted to invest my time into. I watched hours of YouTube videos, bought materials like backdrops and lights. I began to realize photography was more than just the technical to me though. It became about capturing the truth and bearing witness to what I'm seeing in that space in time.

Upon arriving to the University of Maryland, I joined the Black Explosion newspaper where I was able to experiment with the work I was producing and explore my art form in a way that was informative and served the public.

At that same time, I sought to find a way to combine my technical interests in data. Since the results of the 2016 Presidential Election, I was fascinated with how data can be true, yet still fail us at the same time, becoming hyperaware of all the arenas in life that data informed. While at Maryland, I discovered the iSchool where I was able to nurture and develop my skills within data analysis and presentation.

As I approach graduation, I have been able to find innovative ways to combine my talents and desire to visual journalism into my work.
